How does NIST define the term 'least privilege', and why is it important?

3.0314054950140417

Least privilege is a fundamental principle in computer security that states that each entity or process should have only the privileges, access, or permissions necessary to accomplish its intended function. This means that no single component or system can be given more power than needed to perform its tasks.

The importance of least privilege lies in several areas:

1. **Reducing Security Risks**: By limiting the scope of an entity's privileges, organizations can minimize the attack surface and reduce the likelihood of a successful attack.
2. **Improving System Integrity**: Least privilege helps ensure that system components operate independently and do not interfere with each other or with external systems.
3. **Enhancing User Privileges**: Allowing users to perform tasks they are authorized to do enhances their productivity while maintaining security.
4. **Facilitating Auditing and Monitoring**: With least privilege, administrators can monitor system activity without compromising user privileges, making it easier to detect and respond to potential security incidents.

In general, the term "least privilege" is often used interchangeably with "minimizing privileges," but they are not exactly synonymous.

**NIST Definition:**

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) defines least privilege as:

"The principle that no single computer system or program should have access to resources, data, or capabilities beyond what is necessary for the function it performs."

This definition emphasizes the importance of limiting privileges to only those that are needed to accomplish a specific task.

**Key Considerations:**

When implementing least privilege, organizations should consider the following:

* Identify critical systems and services
* Assign minimum necessary permissions and access rights
* Limit access to sensitive data and resources
* Regularly review and update access controls as system requirements change

By embracing the principle of least privilege, organizations can create more secure, reliable, and efficient systems that meet their needs while minimizing risks.

Explain the purpose of security categorization according to NIST.

Security categorization is a process used to classify information types based on their sensitivity and impact levels, which are determined by the potential risks associated with accessing or using that information. According to NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology), security categorization is a critical component of the National Information Security Program (NISP).

The purpose of security categorization is to:

1. Identify the security objectives for each information type
2. Determine the maximum acceptable level of risk associated with accessing or using that information
3. Develop an initial baseline set of security controls to mitigate those risks
4. Monitor and update security controls as needed

Security categories are assigned based on the following criteria:

* The potential harm caused by unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of the information
* The extent to which the information affects national security, law enforcement, or intelligence activities
* The level of risk associated with processing, storing, and maintaining the information

There are four main categories in NIST SP 800-60:

1. Confidential: Information that requires a high level of security due to its sensitivity and potential harm if accessed or used without authorization.
2. Integrity: Information that requires a low level of security to ensure accuracy, reliability, and availability.
3. Availability: Information that requires a moderate level of security to ensure that it is accessible and usable when needed.

Security categories are used to develop a baseline set of security controls for each information type and then monitor and update those controls as needed to maintain the required security level.

In summary, security categorization is an essential process in information security management that helps identify, classify, and manage risks associated with sensitive information. It ensures that security controls are developed and implemented to mitigate those risks and protect sensitive information from unauthorized access or use.
