How does NIST define the term 'least privilege', and why is it important?

3.0876496639102697

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) defines the term "least privilege" as "the minimum level of access to information necessary for a user to perform their job function. It includes all users with authorized access to system data, who need only read or execute access to that data without write or delete access."

According to NIST, implementing least privilege is essential because it:

1. **Prevents unauthorized access**: By limiting access to sensitive information, organizations can prevent unauthorized individuals from accessing confidential data.
2. **Protects against insider threats**: Least privilege helps to mitigate the risk of insider threats by ensuring that users with authorized access only have read or execute permissions on the data they need.
3. **Improves incident response**: With least privilege in place, organizations can respond more effectively to security incidents if an unauthorized user gains access to sensitive information.

To implement least privilege, NIST recommends:

1. **Define roles and responsibilities**: Clearly define job functions and the required level of access for each role.
2. **Assign privileges based on need-to-know**: Grant access only to users who have a legitimate need to access the data they need.
3. **Monitor and control access**: Regularly review and update access permissions to ensure that changes do not compromise security.

By following these guidelines, organizations can implement least privilege effectively and protect themselves against various types of threats.

4. **Monitor and maintain security**: Continuously monitor and update the security categorization and access controls as necessary to ensure they remain effective.
