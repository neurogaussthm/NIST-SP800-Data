6. **Treat Risks**: Develop strategies to manage risks based on their evaluated severity and likelihood. This could involve implementing controls to reduce vulnerability (e.g., patch management), mitigating threats through technical solutions like firewalls or intrusion detection systems, transferring risk via insurance policies, accepting it if the risk is deemed acceptable given cost constraints, or avoiding activities that pose significant risks.

7. **Monitor and Update**: Continuously monitor identified assets, vulnerabilities, threats, and their relationships to ensure risk levels remain within acceptable boundaries over time. 2. **Compliance and Governance**: Adhering to least privilege principles often helps organizations meet regulatory requirements (like HIPAA, PCI DSS) that mandate restrictive access controls. It also aids in maintaining accountability and audit trails by clearly defining who has what permissions.
3. **Operational Efficiency**: By limiting unnecessary privileges, organizations can reduce the risk of insider threats or negligent employees causing harm due to overly broad access rights."""
