Explain the difference between 'identification' and 'authentication' according to NIST guidance.



Answer from granite3.2-NIST6 (1.2650676253251731): 
""" In accordance with NIST Special Publication (SP) 800-63B, 'Identification' refers to the process of uniquely representing an individual, device, or service within a system. This could involve assigning a unique identifier such as a username, email address, or cryptographic key. Identification is typically done to establish a point of reference for subsequent authentication attempts (NIST SP 800-63B, Section 4.1).

Once authenticated, NIST SP 800-63B emphasizes that this process should provide assurance up to the 'acceptable' risk level (Section 5.1).

In summary, while identification is about uniquely identifying an entity within a system, authentication is about validating that identified entity's claimed identity with appropriate levels of confidence or certainty."""
