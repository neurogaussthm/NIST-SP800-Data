Answer from granite3.1-NIST2 (2.329710768070072): 
""" The risk assessment process outlined in NIST Special Publication (SP) 800-30, Revision 1, consists of several key steps: 

1. **Identify Assets**: Determine what information systems and resources are critical to the organization's operations, including both physical and logical assets. These could range from data centers and servers to customer records or intellectual property.

2. **Determine Threats**: Identify potential threats that could harm these assets. This involves understanding the motivations of adversaries (e.g., hackers, insiders) and their capabilities to cause disruption or unauthorized access. Threats can be categorized as intentional (e.g., cyberattacks) or unintentional (e.g., employee errors).

3. **Evaluate Vulnerabilities**: Assess the weaknesses in security controls that could allow threats to exploit assets. This step requires understanding the current state of an organization's security posture, including its technical safeguards, policies, procedures, and human factors. Common methods for evaluating vulnerabilities include penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, and review of security logs.

4. **Determine Impact**: Quantify or qualitatively describe the potential consequences if threats successfully exploited identified vulnerabilities in assets. This involves estimating financial loss, damage to reputation, operational disruption, compliance penalties, and other impacts that could affect the organization's mission or business continuity.

5. **Analyze Risks**: Combine threat information (likelihood of occurrence), vulnerability data (exploitability), and estimated impact into a risk score or composite risk measure. This step may involve using qualitative or quantitative methods, such as simple additive models or more sophisticated techniques like expected monetary value calculations.

6. **Prioritize Risks**: Rank identified risks based on their calculated scores to focus limited resources where they will have the most significant impact. This could involve setting a threshold for acceptable risk levels and focusing efforts only on those risks exceeding that threshold."""
