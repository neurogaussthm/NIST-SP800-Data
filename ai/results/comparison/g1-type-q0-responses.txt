2. Simplifying security management: When each user and process has minimal permissions, it becomes easier to monitor and manage access rights, ensuring compliance with organizational policies.
3. Reducing the attack surface: With fewer entry points into a system due to reduced privileges, there are less potential vulnerabilities for attackers to exploit.

5. Simplifying compliance with regulatory requirements and best practices: Many industry standards and regulations, such as NIST's SP 800-171, require the implementation of the principle of least privilege for enhanced protection of sensitive information.
